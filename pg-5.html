The Farmhouse

I glanced between the house and my friends. This whole thing felt uncomfortable to me.

With a heavy sigh, and wheeled my bike forward.

"o-okay..let's go."

Steph smiled widely, and patted my shoulder.

"Alright Mel. Let's do this!!"


We all got our bikes together, and stared at the house ahead.

It taunted us with its presence.

Steph rode up with excitement with the rest of us trailing behind.

The closer we got to the house, the darker the world seemed to get.

Standing by the front porch, we could see the age of the house. Mold climbed up the wooden panels.

The flooring was splintered, and worped from water damage.

The glass windows were cracked from the shifting house over years of settling.

I immediantly began to regret this whole thing. And I wasn't alone.

Niyanna raised a hand to the house. "Okay..we're here. We saw. Now we leave."

She went to turn, but Chelsea stopped her.

"Come on Ni. We're already here. We'll just take a quick peak, and then leave."

Niyanna huffed, "Fine, but if anything happens in here, I'm kicking your asses"


We chuckled softly as to lighten the mood, but the looming thread still stood.

Steph cleared her throat and climbed off her bike. "Welp, while we're still young, right?"

We each slowly got off our bikes one by one; Chelsea, Niyanna, and Eventually me.

Steph climbed up the steps, which creeked under her weight. She looked around, spotting dead bugs, and webs.

"Ugh, this place is gross."

Chelsea climbed the stairs. "Well you're the one who wanted to come up here."

"Yeah, but it's still gross"

Niyanna climbed up. "Let's just get this over with."

I followed behind, glancing around. Most of me was scared out of my mind, but a part of me found this to be interesting.


Steph reached out and grabbed the door knob, which startled Chelsea.

"What the hell are you doing?!"

"What's it look like I'm doing?"

Chelsea looked irritated. "It looks like you're trying to break in"

Steph turned the knob, which resisted for a moment before turning. The door made an unsettling creak.

Steph applied some weight, and pushed the door open. The door groaned loudly as its bottom grinded against the flooring.

We all stared into the abyss of what seemed to be the main hallway. It was difficult to see what was inside.

There were outlines of what seemed to be cupboards, and drawers. Dust covered every inch, many of which floated in the air.

Steph broke the silence with a smug look to Chelsea. "Breaking in, huh?"

Chelsea got defensive. "Wha-? You think I knew it would just open like that?"

Niyanna tilted her head slightly, "looks abandonned."

I nodded in agreement. It didn't look like anyone had used this home in years.


Steph took a step forward. "Alright. Come on."

Niyanna's eyebrows raised in alert. "Uh, excuse me?

Steph glanced back to the rest of us, who looked uncertain. She scoffed.

"Oh come on, guys! We agreed on this. Besides, do you see this dust? No one has been here in like, ever."

Chelsea took a step forward, glancing around the frame of the door. "We know, it's just...I dunno. It has weird vibes."

I met glances with Niyanna, who shrugged. She stepped forward besides Steph who looked excited.

With a heavy sigh, I stepped in behind them. I had the sickening feeling that I was going to regret this.

The front door is still open. This felt wrong. Maybe it was stories told about this place, but I didn't like this.

This place smelt old, and damp, like when you leave wet clothes in the washer for too long.

Steph was already poking her head into the livingroom. Soft awes came from her through her exploration.

I could turn back...
