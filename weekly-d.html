Weekly: d

09-24-2023

This week has been...slow at best. At least in the way of classwork. The poem I worked with was something I wrote in 2019, during a rough time of my life, and using poety to speak my mind. This was one of mildest of poems, and probably is one of my faves as it speaks of my time when the chaos didn't matter. All that mattered was I was having fun with my best friend, which I hadn't done in a while that that point.

In other news, I got to attend the Renissance Fair with some friends which was a lot of fun. The area felt very "Middle ages" which was the whole point. There were a lot of people who dressed up. Some in plaid, some in armour, others in blown-up dragon outfits. It was a lot of fun, with amazing artworks, craftings, and of course, the food. They sold a variety of food from Turkey leggs to Crepes. I did think about drinking alcohol, but two things blocked me; types of alcohol and long lines. As with any event, lines can be a nightmare, which is why I didn't really go for it. And yes...it's a bummer. I wish...but that's okay. I still had fun.

In the news of work, things we rather interesting. As in Sign Shop - the section of the CABS that works with stops for the buses, and shuttles - where someone had broken one of the windows at a sheltered stop on 11th ave. What was awful was the fact that the glass was on the verge of shattering. And by shattering I mean, crumbling into pieces. It took me nearly 2 hours to sweep up alone. It sucked, but hey...it's work.
